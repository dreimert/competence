TC-3-T-SIS
Télécommunications : Signaux et Systèmes

Ce module présente les bases théoriques et méthodologiques fondamentales pour l'étude des systèmes de communication. Les notions étudiées permettent la caractérisation d'un système continu ou discret d'un point de vue "signal" par des analyses effectuées dans les domaines temporel et fréquentiel. Les outils convolution et corrélation sont manipulés. Les transformées de Fourier, Laplace et Z, sont étudiées. Les systèmes linéaires et temporellement invariants sont analysés à travers les caractéristiques de leur réponse impulsionnelle et fréquentielle.  CAPACITES * Caractériser en temps et en fréquence un « signal déterministe » et « Système Linéaire Temporellement Invariant (SLTI)» * Choisir la représentation fréquentielle la plus adaptée au signal à étudier et analyser ce signal en temps et en fréquence * Calculer la sortie d'un SLTI
---
Telecommunications: Signals and Systems

This module presents the fundamental theoretical and methodological bases for the study of communication systems. The concepts studied allow the characterization of a continuous or discrete system from a "signal" point of view by analyzes carried out in the time and frequency domains. The convolution and correlation tools are manipulated. Fourier transforms, Laplace and Z, are studied. The linear and temporally invariant systems are analyzed through the characteristics of their impulse and frequency response. CAPACITIES * Characterize in time and in frequency a "deterministic signal" and "Linear System Temporarily Invariant (SLTI)" * Choose the frequency representation most adapted to the signal to study and analyze this signal in time and frequency * Calculate the output of a SLTI

TC-5-ARC
Réseaux & Services : Analyse de Risque en sécurité de l'information

A travers l'étude de deux méthodologies d'analyse de risques en sécurité de l'information , cette option vous propose de vous guider pas à pas et selon une pédagogie orientée projet , vers la conception d'une architecture de sécurité issue d'une démarche rationnelle d'analyse des risques , car au final une architecture de sécurité et ses différentes composantes techniques, organisationnelles et économiques ne sont que l'expression de mesures de sécurité visant à réduire voir supprimer les risques que cours le système d'information. TC-3-R-PRS
Réseaux & Services : Programmation, Réseau et Système

L'objectif principal de ce module est de présenter par la théorie et la pratique le fonctionnement de la couche transport dans un réseau de communications. Des notions fondamentales sont approfondies, comme le contrôle de flux et le contrôle de congestion de bout-en-bout. PRS vise deux objectifs importants: 1) l'étude théorique et pratique de différents mécanismes de la couche transport, et 2) la proposition d'une couche transport adaptée à un contexte spécifique. Un outil essentiel pour réaliser ces deux objectifs est représenté par la programmation réseau en utilisant l'API Sockets, ce qui rend la maîtrise de cette interface de programmation comme troisième objectif majeur du module.  CONNAISSANCES * Couche transport: rôle, mécanismes de base, services offerts * Les protocoles TCP et UDP * Les notions de contrôle de flux et contrôle de congestion * Concept de socket * Appels système * Architecture client-serveur  CAPACITÉS * Concevoir et développer une couche réseau transport adaptée à des scénarios donnés * Implanter des architectures réseau en utilisant l'API Sockets
---
Networks & Services: Programming, Network and System

The main objective of this module is to present by theory and practice the operation of the transport layer in a communications network. Fundamentals are in-depth, such as flow control and end-to-end congestion control. PRS has two important objectives: 1) the theoretical and practical study of different mechanisms of the transport layer, and 2) the proposal of a transport layer adapted to a specific context. An essential tool for achieving both of these goals is represented by network programming using the Sockets API, which makes mastering this programming interface the third major objective of the module. KNOWLEDGE * Transport layer: role, basic mechanisms, services offered * TCP and UDP protocols * The concepts of flow control and congestion control * Socket concept * System calls * Client-server architecture CAPABILITIES * Design and develop a network layer transport adapted to given scenarios * Implement network architectures using the Sockets API

IST-4-DBM2
Des bases de donnés à la fouille de données - Partie2 : fouille de : données

This course introduces data mining as an extension of standard database querying approaches. We motivate the need for new querying mechanisms to support data analysis and Knowledge Discovery from Databases (KDD) by means of data mining techniques. The classical data mining algorithms (clustering, classification, association rule mining) are introduced. We explain why it is interesting to study and implement such algorithms within the recent setting of constraint-based data mining. Finally, the design of data mining query languages is considered. To be concrete, the students will have to perform some practical data analysis tasks by means of the open source KNIME data mining tool. Beside the introduction of the most popular techniques, we will do our best to sketch research challenges in the area, including a discussion on the research results collected in Lyon during the last decade.

TC-4-T-TIP
Télécommunications : Traitement de signaux numériques : Parole et Image

L'objectif de ce module est de présenter les méthodes de base en traitement de la parole, des images et des vidéos, ainsi que les normes de compression associées. Le module est illustré par de nombreuses études, notamment en images, en analyse vidéo (reconnaissance d'action, suivi d'objets) et reconnaissance de visages.  Ce module comporte une première partie formelle sur les bases théoriques et méthodologiques permettant la caractérisation d'un signal d'un point de vue "mathématique" dans l'espace de Hilbert.  COMPETENCES * Caractéristiques principales des espaces de représentations des signaux et des images * Approximation optimales des signaux et des images * Compréhension de la transformée en ondelettes et de l'analyse multi-résolution * Techniques de codage parole, audio, image 2D et vidéo * Traitements de base pour les images  CAPACITES * Modéliser un signal continu ou discret par un signal approximant * Mettre en oeuvre les techniques d'analyse multirésolution des signaux * Modéliser le système phonatoire et le système auditif pour analyser et synthétiser la parole et l'audio * Concevoir une chaîne de traitement d'images simple * Concevoir une chaîne de compression de la parole, de l'audio, des images et des vidéos
---
Telecommunications: Digital Signal Processing: Word and Image

The objective of this module is to present the basic methods of speech, image and video processing, as well as the associated compression standards. The module is illustrated by numerous studies, including images, video analysis (action recognition, object tracking) and face recognition. This module includes a first formal part on the theoretical and methodological bases for the characterization of a signal from a "mathematical" point of view in the Hilbert space. SKILLS * Key features of signal and image representation spaces * Optimum approximation of signals and images * Understanding of wavelet transform and multi-resolution analysis * Speech, audio, 2D image and video coding techniques * Treatments base for images CAPABILITIES * Model a continuous or discrete signal with an approximate signal * Implement multi-resolution signal analysis techniques * Model the speech system and the auditory system to analyze and synthesize speech and audio * Design a simple image processing chain * Design a compression chain of speech, audio, images and videos

IST-4-DBM1
Des bases de donnés à la fouille de données - Partie1 : bases de : données

This course introduces the most important concepts concerning the design and the use of databases. It covers the main issues in data modeling when considering the relational data model (structure, languages like relational algebra and relational calculus) and the key features of attribute-oriented models with respect to constructor-oriented models (like, for instance, Extended Entity-Relationship models). The fundamental concepts like the constraints and the normal forms will be explained and their impact on the quality of designed databases will be discussed. Finally, practical aspects of relational database design and use will be discussed. It includes a presentation of the SQL query language but also a short introduction to data warehousing and On Line Analytical Processing queries (OLAP). To be concrete, the students will have to understand the design of a real relational database and to practice database querying with a professional DataBase Management System (DBMS).

Projets : Gestion de projets

Gérer un projet est une activité standard dans l'ingénérie. Cependant, la majorité des projets n'aboutissent pas à leur terme ni dans les objectifs visés, ni dans les délais, ni dans les coûts estimés. Cette matière couvre les enjeux liés à la notion de projet ; et porte un focus spécifique sur des outils agiles récents pour leur conduite. Projects: Project Management

Managing a project is a standard activity in engineering. However, the majority of projects do not reach completion, either in terms of their objectives, timeframes, or estimated costs. This subject covers issues related to the notion of project; and focuses specifically on recent agile tools for their conduct. Skills * Know how to define the contours of a project * Manage a project in cycles * Manage a project continuously Knowledge * Agile games * Scrum * Kanban

IST-4-OPS
Informatique : Systèmes d'exploitation

The aim of the course is to present the fundamental concepts and issues in the topic of operating Systems. We will first take a short historical tour of computer systems and discuss why operating systems were introduced. We will then describe the major components and abstractions of a general-purpose OS. Finally, we will address the programming interfaces of many operating systems based on processes, various interprocess communication techniques and scheduling of processes.

Computer Science: Operating Systems

The aim of the race is to present the fundamental concepts and issues in the operating systems. We will first take a short historical tour of computer systems and discuss why operating systems were introduced. We will describe the major components and abstractions of a general-purpose OS. Finally, we will address the programming of many operating systems based on processes, various interprocess communication techniques and scheduling of processes.

Physical Education and Sports: EC Physical and Sports Activities

The Physical Education and Sports Education program at INSA Lyon is part of the cross-curricular framework of Humanities, EPS and Documentation. A goal: to contribute to the training, through the university practice of Physical Sports and Artistic Activities, of the engineer of tomorrow: a responsible and responsible citizen, able to communicate, to participate in group dynamics, to innovate and to adapt. The training is based on 3 axes: - Self-management - Management of others - Management of a sports project Among the academic disciplines, the EPS is the sole guarantor for the students of a corporal commitment of the first to the fifth year of their course. For many of them it represents the only moment of physical activity and for all of them it must bring knowledge, capacities and attitudes allowing them to build, then maintain, throughout their life, their health habits. Through the search for targeted skills (technical and motor, intrapersonal, relational and organizational), this teaching falls within the 5 priority competencies of the Humanities and PSE training policy for all students regardless of their background. (International classics, exchange students, exchange students and students unfit to practice) Through complex problem solving situations, the student engineer is confronted with the motor and body experience, the development of a global intelligence involving the full mobilization of resources: motor, cognitive, relational, informational and emotional.
